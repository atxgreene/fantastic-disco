Personal Portfolio Brand System

Core positioning: Austin-rooted operator, AI systems builder, endurance athlete, and independent researcher turning messy problems into useful operating systems.

Operator Builder Researcher Hybrid Athlete Local-first AI

Brand Essence

One-liner: Building the personal operating system for ambition, useful AI, and human performance.

Personality: disciplined, direct, curious, technical, slightly mythic, Austin/Texas grounded, with enough humor to feel human.

Vibe: Apple-clean portfolio meets Palantir mission-control board, with a little endurance-race grit and ATX warmth.

Tagline Options

  • Primary: Build the edge. Keep the soul.
  • Technical: Systems for ambition, memory, and momentum.
  • Portfolio: Operator. Builder. Researcher.
  • Personal: Why not me?
  • Mythic: A wannabe Icarus, building better wings.

Typography

Display: Space Grotesk — for headings, nav, project titles.

Body: Inter or SF Pro — for clean portfolio readability.

Code/Data: IBM Plex Mono — for project specs, coordinates, changelogs, terminal blocks.

Logo Concept

Mark: circular ATX monogram with teal ring, orange accent, and partial laurel/orbit arcs.

Meaning: ATX roots, operator loop, endurance cycle, AI systems orbit, and personal growth.

Use: monogram for favicon/social avatar; full lockup for hero section, résumé header, GitHub profile readme, and personal landing page.

Voice Rules

  • Lead with outcomes and earned competence.
  • Write like an operator: clear, direct, specific.
  • Use mythic language sparingly as flavor, not costume.
  • Make AI feel practical, local-first, useful, and human.

Homepage Sections

  • Hero: one-liner, CTA, current build focus.
  • Projects: B.E.N., Mnemosyne, Tugboat, Snakepit, Bluebonnet.
  • Operating principles: local-first, auditable, human-centered.
  • Personal edge: endurance, dogs, Austin roots, humor.

Do

  • Use dark navy backgrounds with teal/orange highlights.
  • Use monospaced labels for system/project details.
  • Show projects as operating systems, not random experiments.
  • Use photos/art that feel field-tested, technical, and personal.

Don’t

  • Overdo neon cyberpunk or generic AI gradients.
  • Make it too corporate or too meme-heavy.
  • Use orange as the dominant color; it should be the signal.
  • Hide the human parts: dogs, racing, Austin roots, humor.
